    Who is Jellybean Benítez?

    Jellybean Benítez is an American music producer, songwriter, DJ, drummer, guitarist, and remixer who is known for his work in Carlito’s Way (1993), Species (1995), and Call Me by Your Name.

    Furthermore, he is known for producing and remixing artists such as Michael Jackson, Whitney Houston, Madonna, and Pointer Sisters.

    Jellybean Benítez: Age, Parents, Ethnicity

    Jellybean was born on November 7, 1957, in The Bronx, New York City, New York, USA.

    He is 63 years old.

    However, his father and mother are from Puerto Rico so he is of Puerto Rico descent. He is of American nationality.

    He was raised and brought up solely by his mother with his younger sibling.

    At an early age, he grew up listening to his sister’s (Debbie) record collections.

    Education

    Furthermore, he studied Marketing and Sales Promotion at Bronx Community College.

    Jellybean Benítez: Professional life, Career

    Jellybean Benítez started his career as Disc Jockey(DJ) in 1975 in various nightclubs including Electric Circus, Hurrah, Xenon, Paradise Garage, and Studio 54.

    Later he started to remix singles for Jimmy Spicer, Afrika Bambaataa, and Stephen Bray, and gave pop singer, Madonna her start through her debut album”Madonna” in 1983, including the singles “Everybody”, “Borderline”, and “Lucky Star.”

    He has worked with numerous artists such as Whitney Houston, Michael Jackson, Sting, Benson, Hall and Oates, James Ingram, The Pointer Sisters, and Paul McCartney among others.

    Among the many hits, his many topping charts released in the 1980s include “Sidewalk Talk” (US #18 and the UK #47), “Who Found Who” (US #16 and UK #10), “The Real Thing” (UK #13), and “Just a Mirage” (UK #13) among the others.

    Furthermore, he has a music company under his name,’ Jellybean Music Group‘, and is the producer of Studio 54 Radio.

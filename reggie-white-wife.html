    Throughout his life, Reggie had a special connection with his family. He married his longtime partner Sara White in January 1985, and they were together for almost 19 years until Reggie’s untimely death.

    Sara and Reggie first met in church when she moved to Knoxville, Tennessee, as a high school junior, and he was a college freshman. They became close and started dating. In an interview after Reggie’s death, Sara shared how she cherished every moment with her late husband.

    Gridiron Glory: Analyzing Reggie White’s NFL Stats and Career Honors!!

    Reggie White started playing professional football with the Memphis Showboats in 1984 in the USFL. Unfortunately, the league closed in 1985.

    After almost two years in the USFL, White joined the Philadelphia Eagles. He played with them for eight years before moving to the Green Bay Packers.

    White played with the Packers for six years and helped them win the Super Bowl in 1997. In 2000, he joined the Carolina Panthers for a year before retiring from football.

    In his 15-year NFL career, White achieved 312 tackles, 198 sacks, 29 pass breakups, 14 forced fumbles, 12 fumbles recovered, and three interceptions. 

    He received the Defensive Player of the Year award twice and was selected for the Pro Bowl 13 times. Reggie White was honored with induction into the Hall of Fame in 2006.

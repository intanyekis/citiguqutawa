    Cody Ware Career

    Cody Ware Career: - Ware won Rookie of the Year honors in the Lamborghini Super Trofeo North America class in 2014. He made his NASCAR Nationwide Series debut in August 2014 at the Mid-Ohio Sports Car Course, starting 26th and finishing 15th for RWR. On November 23, 2019, Ware revealed that he would participate in EuroInternational in the 2020 24 Hours of Le Mans. Ware joined MAKE Motorsports full-time in 2015 for the NASCAR Camping World Truck Series season, where he competed for Rookie of the Year honors. In the Folds of Honor QuikTrip 500 in Atlanta, he drove the No. 51, which was sponsored by Spoonful of Music and Bubba Burger.

    Ware switched to motorcycle racing in 2018 but came back to the Cup Series for RWR at Dover. In 2019, Ware upped his Cup schedule to 13 races, including the Daytona 500, when he and RWR teammate B. J. McLeod wrecked as multiple cars were entering the pit road, leading him to finish 39th. His one 2020 Cup start occurred at Talladega Superspeedway in the YellaWood 500. RWR announced on January 18, 2021, that Ware would drive the No. 51 vehicle for the entire 2021 Cup season. Ware moved to the No. 51 in 2022, beginning with a 17th-place result in the Daytona 500. For 2023, Ware arrived at the No. 51, beginning with a 14th-place performance in the Daytona 500. On June 15, it was confirmed that Ware will make his IndyCar Series debut in the No. 52 entry at the REV Group Grand Prix at Road America.

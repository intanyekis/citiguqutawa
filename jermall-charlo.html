    Jermall Charlo Boxing Career

    Charlo started boxing from his early days when he started to follow in his father’s footsteps. He was fortunate enough to get a spot on the 2008 U.S. Olympic team, but he couldn’t continue to play due to a toe injury. He finished his first amateur year with a record of sixty-five wins and six losses.

    Charlo started his professional boxing appearance in 2008 and competed against Cimmaron Davis and won the fight via second-round technical knockout. Jermall, in his light-middleweight year, competed against many champions including Cornelius Bundrage, Wilky Campfort, Austin Trout, and Julian Williams.

    On February 16, 2017, Charlo officially left his IBF light middleweight title to move up to middleweight. On March 11, World Boxing Council (WBC) ranked Jermall number two ahead of David Lemieux, and Curtis Stevens and the International Boxing Federation (IBF) ranked him on number three.

    Charlo fought against the world’s greatest boxers in the middleweight championships. He competed and won against Jorge Sebastian Heiland, Hugo Centeno, and Barclays Center. In his professional boxing records, he has 28 fights out of which 21 were by knockout and seven were by decision. He had 28 wins and 0 losses in his professional record summary.

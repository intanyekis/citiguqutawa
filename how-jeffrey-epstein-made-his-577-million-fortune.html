    Epstein's Unorthodox Career Trajectory

    Jeffrey Epstein was first known worldwide as a well-connected financier, which helped him accumulate an enormous fortune.

    However, the financial advisor and manager would begin his professional trajectory as a teacher of physics and mathematics at the Dalton School on the Upper East Side of Manhattan ... a disturbing element of his backstory, in retrospect.

    The school eventually dismissed the upcoming icon, and he later found his calling in the financial sector through his friendship with the CEO of the New York City-based investment bank, Bear Stearns.

    Epstein's transition from education to the world of finance would prove pivotal, bringing him immense wealth and prestige before his Earth-shattering downfall.

    Jeffrey Epstein's Career In Finance

    Epstein's companionship with the CEO of the once-powerful, now defunct Bear Stearns, Alan Greenberg, proved to be an integral building block to his steady accumulation of wealth, capital, and his most notable asset – powerful connections.

    Epstein started off as a junior assistant to a floor trader in the company, but swiftly moved up the ranks to the position of "financial advisor," servicing some of the bank's wealthiest clients.

    Epstein's association with the likes of the president of the Canadian multinational conglomerate Seagrams made him a prominent figure in the banking sector, with his expertise on "tax mitigation" strategies earning him praise from subsequent Bear Stearns CEO Jimmy Cayne.

    Epstein's association with the bank would turn out to be short-lived, as the emerging financial mogul was found guilty of a "Reg D" violation.

    That trading violation concluded his stint as a financial advisor for the once-influential bank and its moneyed clients.

    Epstein Starts His Own Financial Management Firm

    After his stint as a financial consultant, Jeffrey Epstein had garnered enough knowledge and experience in the financial sector.

    Epstein launched his own financial consulting firm in 1981, and associated himself with the debt collection firm "Towers Financial Corporation" in 1987, which further bolstered his credibility as a financial consultant.

    Inside Epstein's Extravagant Spending

    After establishing a thriving financial career, Jeffrey Epstein was able to access some of the most exorbitant materialistic commodities that money can buy.

    The disgraced financier would make headlines across the globe for his acquisition of the Little Saint James in 1998, as well as the island Great Saint James in 2016; their most recent valuation stood at a staggering $125 million.

    Epstein's Little Saint James property, spanning 72 acres, boasted a private helipad, four guesthouses, a private dock, a gas station, and a private main residence. Epstein's famed island was one of the most well-equipped and notorious destinations on the planet.

    Additionally, the island is home to three private beaches, while the general aura of serenity surrounding the private property served as a perfect getaway destination for a bevy of reported aristocrats hailing from across the globe.

    Biography:
    Moneybagg Yo was born on September 22, 1991, in Memphis, Tennessee, United States. He grew up in the city’s South Memphis neighborhood. Moneybagg Yo began his music career in 2012, initially using the name Lil Yo. He gained local recognition through his mixtapes, which helped him establish a solid fan base.

    In 2016, Moneybagg Yo gained wider attention with his mixtape “Federal,” which featured popular tracks like “All Gas No Brakes” and “Real Me.” He followed it up with several successful mixtapes, including “Heartless” and “2 Federal.” His unique style, blending trap beats with introspective lyrics, resonated with listeners and helped him build a dedicated following.

    In 2017, Moneybagg Yo signed a record deal with Yo Gotti’s record label, Collective Music Group (CMG), and Interscope Records. His debut studio album, “Reset,” was released in November 2018 and featured collaborations with artists like Future, Kevin Gates, and J. Cole. The album received positive reviews and debuted at number 13 on the Billboard 200 chart.

    Since then, Moneybagg Yo has continued to release successful projects, including the albums “43va Heartless” (2019) and “Time Served” (2020). He has collaborated with numerous prominent artists such as Megan Thee Stallion, Lil Baby, and Polo G, among others.
